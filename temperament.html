Theory: Polarity and chemistry

Temperament here is distinct from biological sex or gender identity. It refers to archetypal energy patterns and behavioral tendencies on a spectrum. Variation across dimensions is normal. Understanding your temperament clarifies relationship polarity, compatibility dynamics, and personal development—and when your position suggests considering partner fit.

Complementary poles by degree: Lasting chemistry and mutual support often involve occupying opposite poles on the same dimensions with matched strength—not only “opposite,” but opposite at a similar level of intensity. The spectrum dots in your report are population references, not targets; fit is about complementarity, not both matching the same trend.

Contextual framing: Temperament reflects how energy tends to organize in relational and intimate contexts. It varies by partner, season, safety, and life phase. Results describe tendencies and preferences, not fixed identity or mandates.
