💑 Relationships

Score your relationship across key dimensions to identify strengths, strain points and viability.

Extended Explanation

The assessment runs in two stages, in order. First, Points of Conflict maps strain across 20 compatibility areas—ranked pressure points, targeted strategies, and archetypal dynamics where relevant. Then Relationship Viability examines six considerations and yields an overall band: whether repair-focused investment fits your situation or stepping away is clearer.

Paradigm context

This assessment sits in the modern dating and relationship evaluation (unplugged / red-pill) framework: realistic, no-holds-barred appraisal of relational strain, viability, and sexual-market context. It is a structural read for clearer decisions, not fault assignment or a command to stay/leave.

Assessment flow

  • Part 1 — Points of Conflict (three phases): broad compatibility scan, domain deep dive on strain points, then scenario-based reflection
  • Part 2 — Relationship Viability: six dimensions with an overall guidance band (worth investing / unclear / consider stepping away)

What you'll discover

  • After Part 1: 20 compatibility areas with impact tiers; strain ranked by priority and severity; archetypal pressures where relevant; action strategies (self-regulation, invitations, boundaries, change vs. acceptance) where specific
  • After Part 2: dimension-level read and overall viability band, plus reflection prompts aligned with your answers
  • Closure and next steps; save a readable HTML report for AI-assisted review, printing, or PDF export

Theory: Strain points and viability

This engine identifies pressure points in relational structure. It does not determine fault or whether a relationship should continue—it reflects your experience. Part 1 surfaces strain and strategies; Part 2 applies the six viability considerations to whether investing in resolution is likely to bring long-term value. Draws on relationship dynamics, polarity, and mating-market framing.

Disclaimer

Descriptive self-assessment only—not diagnosis, prediction, therapy, medical, or legal advice. A structural read for sovereign self-inquiry: strain mapping and viability calibration from your answers—not fault-finding, and not a directive to stay or leave. Reads reflect the current moment and can shift as the relationship, context, or you change; use them as one input among many, not as a permanent label.

Important Note

Use this for structural clarity—strain points, strategies, and a viability band. It does not score character depth; it maps how the dynamic reads from your reported experience.

Treat outputs as calibration for decision-making, not as ammunition for ultimatums—combine with context you already hold.
