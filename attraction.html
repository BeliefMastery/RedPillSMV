Theory: Red-pill attraction and status

Three undercurrents: Coalition Rank is intrasex standing. Reproductive Confidence reflects child-rearing demand signals (e.g. provision/protection vs. loyalty, nurture, trust). Axis of Attraction is the early, display-heavy filter. Together they model how selection pressure tends to show up in social and dating markets—not a verdict on who you “are” forever.

Male cluster shorthand: 3C’s (Courage, Control, Competence), 4P’s (Perspicacity, Protector, Provider, Parental Investor), Axis (Wealth/Finance/Status/Performance/Productivity/Talent/Popularity; Looks/Physical/Genetic/Aesthetic; Humour/Intelligence/Companionship; Radical Activity—cool/novel outside pursuits, mate competition for attention—as a modifier when notable). Female cluster shorthand: 3S’s (Social Influence, Selectivity, Status Signaling), Reproductive Confidence (Paternity Certainty, Nurturing Standard, Collaborative Trust Efficiency), Axis (Fertility & Health, Risk Cost, Personality, Factors Hidden). Common frames: upward convergence (hypergamy) vs. commitment and stability (pair-bonding).

Radical Activity (men): Treated as a modifier—how cool or novel your pursuits outside romance are, so a partner has benign competition for your attention and the dynamic is less likely to go stale. Heavy consumption/escape reads as little worth competing with; visible skill, risk, or build reads as high signal. The report calls this out when it meaningfully qualifies the core map.

Results describe leverage and configuration, not fixed identity—states are time-sensitive.
